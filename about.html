About Pulsegrid

We started Pulsegrid because dashboards weren't keeping up.

A small team obsessed with making infrastructure understandable, not just visible.

Our story

Built by engineers who got paged too often

Pulsegrid began in 2020 when three infrastructure engineers got tired of staring at a dozen disconnected dashboards during every incident, trying to piece together what had actually gone wrong.

Instead of building another metrics graph, we set out to build a system that understands the relationships between services, so when something breaks, you see the cause, not just a wall of red numbers.

Today, Pulsegrid commits to provide real-time alerting and monitoring to the engineering teams to turn raw telemetry into decisions they can act on in seconds.

What we believe

The principles behind every feature we ship

Clarity over noise

A dashboard nobody can read at 3 a.m. during an outage isn't actually helpful. We design for the worst moments, not the demo.

Trust through transparency

We show our reasoning, not just a verdict. Every alert explains why it fired and what changed.

Built to be outgrown

We'd rather you outgrow a feature than be locked into one. Our tools stay simple as your systems get complex.
